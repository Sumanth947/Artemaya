**1. Personalized Diet Overview**

* **Location:** Kochi, Kerala.  The plan will heavily utilize Kerala's staple ingredients like matta rice, coconut, turmeric, black pepper, and local vegetables like drumstick, ash gourd, and moringa leaves.

* **Health Condition:**  No specific health condition mentioned beyond being in the luteal phase. The plan will focus on managing potential PMS symptoms through magnesium-rich foods, complex carbohydrates, and foods that promote mood stability.

* **Menstrual Phase:** Luteal phase (days 16-28).  The focus is on managing PMS symptoms, mood stability, and preparing the body for menstruation.  We will incorporate magnesium-rich foods, complex carbohydrates, and potentially calming spices.


**2. Weekly Meal Plan (Luteal Phase Focus)**

**Day 1:**

* **Breakfast (7:30 AM):**  Ragi porridge with dates and a sprinkle of cardamom (Quick Recipe).
* **Lunch (1:00 PM):**  Kerala Style Avial with Matta rice (Medium Recipe). Include a side of Thoran (using drumstick or ash gourd).
* **Snack (4:00 PM):**  A handful of mixed nuts (almonds, cashews, walnuts) and a small banana.
* **Dinner (8:00 PM):**  Moringa leaf curry with brown rice and a side of steamed vegetables (Quick Recipe).

**Day 2:**

* **Breakfast (7:30 AM):**  Oats porridge with banana and a few chopped nuts.
* **Lunch (1:00 PM):**  Kerala Style Moru Curry with  Matta rice (Quick Recipe).
* **Snack (4:00 PM):**  Sprouts salad with a light lemon dressing.
* **Dinner (8:00 PM):**  Steamed fish (if consuming) with a side of mixed vegetable thoran and brown rice.

**Day 3:**

* **Breakfast (7:30 AM):**  Idli (using a mix of rice and ragi) with coconut chutney.
* **Lunch (1:00 PM):**  Sambar (can adapt to Kerala style using more coconut) and Matta rice. Include a side of a leafy green thoran (e.g., spinach).
* **Snack (4:00 PM):**  A small bowl of curd (yogurt) with a few dates.
* **Dinner (8:00 PM):**  Vegetable stew with appam.

**Day 4-7:** Repeat a similar pattern, varying the vegetables and protein sources (e.g.,  red cow peas, black-eyed peas) to ensure a diverse nutrient intake.  Include dishes like  Puttu with Kadala Curry, or various other Kerala vegetable preparations to avoid monotony.


**3. Shopping List**

* **Essential Ingredients:** Matta rice, coconut (fresh and desiccated), turmeric, black pepper, curry leaves, ginger, garlic, mustard seeds, cumin seeds, green chilies, coconut oil.
* **Weekly Fresh Produce:**  Drumstick, ash gourd, moringa leaves, spinach, banana, dates, mixed nuts, various seasonal vegetables (based on availability and preference),  sprouts (mung beans),  fish (optional).
* **Storage Tips:** Store rice in airtight containers in a cool, dry place.  Fresh coconut can be refrigerated; desiccated coconut can be stored in an airtight container.  Fresh vegetables should be stored in the refrigerator.


**4. Health Recommendations**

* **Menstrual Phase (Luteal):** Focus on magnesium-rich foods (leafy greens, nuts, seeds) and complex carbs (millets, whole grains).  Reduce caffeine and alcohol intake.
* **Condition Management:** No specific conditions mentioned beyond the luteal phase. If PMS symptoms are severe, consider incorporating more magnesium-rich foods and exploring stress-reducing techniques.
* **Supplement Needs:**  If you have any concerns about iron or other nutrient deficiencies, consult your doctor for blood tests and recommendations for supplementation.


**5. Practical Tips**

* **Meal Prep:** Prepare larger batches of curries and thorans on the weekends and store them in the refrigerator for easy weekday meals.  Wash and chop vegetables in advance.
* **Time Management:**  Choose a mix of quick and medium recipes to balance cooking time. Utilize the quick recipes on busy weekdays.
* **Storage Solutions:** Use airtight containers to store leftovers. Freeze larger portions of curries and thorans for later use.
