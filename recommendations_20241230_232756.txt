**3. Shopping List**

* **Essential Ingredients:**  Coconut oil, turmeric powder, black pepper, ginger, garlic, cumin seeds, mustard seeds, curry leaves,  various lentils (toor dal, moong dal, masoor dal), millets (ragi, little jowar).

* **Weekly Fresh Produce:**  Spinach, moringa leaves, drumstick, ash gourd, bananas, dates, seasonal fruits, various vegetables (based on weekly availability and preference).

* **Storage Tips:** Store spices in airtight containers in a cool, dark place.  Store fresh produce in the refrigerator or in a cool, dry place. Wash and chop vegetables for quicker meal prep.


**4. Health Recommendations**

* **Specific to Menstrual Phase:**  Increase iron-rich foods during menstruation, especially during the heavier flow.  Focus on anti-inflammatory foods to reduce pain.

* **Condition Management (PCOD):**  Prioritize millets, leafy greens, legumes, and anti-inflammatory spices.  Limit refined carbs, processed foods, and sugary drinks.  Maintain regular meal timing.

* **Supplement Needs:**  Krishna should consult her doctor about B12 supplementation and iron supplements if needed.  Monitor her iron levels and Vitamin D levels through blood tests.


**5. Practical Tips**

* **Meal Prep Suggestions:**  Dedicate a few hours on the weekend to prepping ingredients. Chop vegetables, cook grains, and prepare basic sauces.  This will save time during the week.

* **Time Management:** Utilize quick recipes during busy weekdays and elaborate recipes on weekends.

* **Storage Solutions:** Invest in airtight containers to store leftover food and prepped ingredients.  Use freezer-safe containers for meal prepping and freezing portions.  Label and date everything for better organization.

Regular monitoring of her weight, blood work (especially iron and B12 levels), and PMS symptoms will help adjust the plan accordingly.  The increased calorie target (to reach 80kg) needs careful monitoring to avoid unhealthy weight gain. Gradual increase is recommended.
